Recommended Tools and Methods
Utilize mobile applications designed for BLE scanning that display signal strength (RSSI) to help gauge proximity. These can be run on a smartphone mounted in a vehicle, ideally operated by a passenger to avoid distractions. Key suggestions include:
•  Wunderfind (Available for iOS and Android): This application scans for nearby Bluetooth devices and provides a visual representation of signal strength, allowing you to identify when a target device comes into range. It is user-friendly for real-time monitoring and can assist in narrowing down locations by noting increases in RSSI as you approach. ￼
•  nRF Connect (Available for Android and iOS): Developed for professional use, this tool offers detailed BLE scanning with RSSI readings and device filtering. It is particularly useful for observing broadcast names or identifiers, enabling you to drive slowly through areas while monitoring for matches. ￼
•  BLE Radar (Available on F-Droid for Android): This open-source application analyzes surrounding BLE traffic, provides alerts for new or suspicious devices, and tracks movement patterns. It supports filters to focus on specific device types, which could aid in detecting consistent signals over time. ￼
•  LightBlue or Bluetooth BLE Device Finder (Available for iOS and Android): These free tools list nearby devices with RSSI indicators, facilitating proximity-based searches. They are straightforward for beginners and can log detections for later review. ￼ ￼
